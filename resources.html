Division of Knowledge Multiplies It

The purpose of the page is to share resources, particularly notes and documents authored by me that may prove to be useful for someone other than myself. Please feel free to give feedback and suggestions for content improvement. I must state, for sake of clarity, that this page is currently meant to share resources whose existence has been possible by my contribution in some way or the other. If you do find the material on this web site useful, it’d be nice if you can drop me a line at this address. It’d go a long way in encouraging me to share more. Thanks!

A List of Resources

Image Reduction and Analysis Facility

I have been teaching IRAF to Master’s level and PhD students for quite some time now. Recently, I realized that oral instructions can be too slow for or too fast for a mixed audience. So I decided to construct a written tutorial to help beginners get a feel for working with IRAF. The tutorials of course assume that you have somehow gotten help and installed IRAF already. Once you have done that, you can follow these tutorials to get started.

  • Introduction to IRAF
  • Basic CCD Reduction with IRAF
  • Quick Look Aperture Photometry with IRAF

An Astro-Ubuntu

I have managed to modify the original ISO of Ubuntu 12.04 LTS to have astronomical software such as IRAF, PyRAF, Python, iPython, astrometry.net and also some astronomy related libraries within Python. Please note that I am not a professional and so the ISO image is yours to try without any assurances. Instructions on how use the image can be obtained here.
